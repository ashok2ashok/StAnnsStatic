Admissions - College of Nursing

Admission to St. Ann’s College of Nursing is based on student’s aggregate score in the qualifying examination (10+2 with science ).


Minimum 45% marks are required in Science and English to apply for this course.

Admissions - School of Nursing


General Nursing and Midwifery course

Admission to St. Ann’s School of Nursing is based on student’s aggregate score in the qualifying examination (10+2 with any subject).

Minimum 45% marks are required in Optionals and English to apply for this course.

St. Ann's School of Nursing, Warangal established in 1998, is recognised by the Board of Examinations for GNM, Directorate of Medical Education, Government of Andhra Pradesh, A.P state Council for Nurses & Midwives, Hyderabad and the Indian Nursing Council, New Delhi.


Number of seats for govt. counseling students

=

30

Number of management seats

=

20

Total

50

Duration of the course : 3 years and 6 months

Applications for three and a half years General Nursing and Midwifery course will be available at this website http://dme.tg.nic.in (tentatively from August 2015)
